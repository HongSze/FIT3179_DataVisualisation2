How does air pollution change over time?

Dashed lines show particulate matter (PM₂.₅ / PM₁₀) measured in µg/m³ on the left axis, while solid lines show gases (CO, NO₂, O₃, SO₂) measured in ppm on the right axis. PM varies more with weather and burning events, while gases behave differently: NO₂ tracks traffic and dips during mobility slowdowns, CO stays low and stable, and O₃ is formed photochemically, tending to peak in sunnier, drier periods rather than during haze.

Which sectors contribute most to greenhouse gas emissions?

Malaysia’s emissions are led by the Energy sector throughout the period, with Waste a distant second and Agriculture/Industrial Processes contributing smaller, steadier shares. Land Use and Forestry (LULUCF) is negative—acting as a carbon sink that offsets part of the total—so changes in forest cover can noticeably shift the net trajectory even when sectoral emissions are fairly stable.
